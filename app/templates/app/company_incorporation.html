Everything You Need to Know About Business Registration in India

Registering a business is a critical step towards establishing a successful enterprise. Whether you're starting as a solo entrepreneur or with co-founders, understanding the different business structures available in India is essential to ensure legal compliance, protect your assets, and build credibility in the market.

  • Private Limited Company
  • Public Limited Company
  • Section 8 Company (NGO / NPO)
  • LLP (Limited Liability Partnership)
  • Non Banking Financial Company (NBFC)
  • Foreign Subsidiary
  • Liaison Office / Branch Office / Project Office
  • One Person Company (OPC)
  • Producer Company
  • Nidhi Company

Once you choose the appropriate business structure, the next steps typically include obtaining the required Digital Signature Certificates (DSC) and Director Identification Numbers (DIN), and filing the necessary incorporation documents. For instance, you may need to file forms like RUN, SPICe+, and submit documents like the Memorandum of Association (MOA) and Articles of Association (AOA).

After successful submission and verification, you will receive the Certificate of Incorporation along with your Company Identification Number (CIN). You can then proceed to apply for PAN, TAN, open a current account, and begin your business operations. In addition, registering for GST and MSME is highly recommended if applicable to your business.
